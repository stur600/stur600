The Leaky Pipedream?

A Bourdieusian Study of Gender Differences in Undergraduate Physics

14th October

Pierre Bourdieu - photograph by Leonardo Antoniadis

  • French Sociologist active around 1960 - 1990

  • Interested in the systems of relations that define society, and how this relates to the distribution of power between groups.

  • Double objectivity - He considered both the objective structure of society and consciousness of agents in the field.

    • Three Key Concepts:

      Field

    • The social world is a multidimensional space that is comprised of many different fields.

    • Each field is hierarchical

      • Capital

    • Economic, Cultural, Social, Symbolic

      • Habitus

    • ``a system of dispositions'' (Bourdieu, 1984)

    • A ``generative schema'' --- a mental structure that embodies societal structures. (Nash, 1999)

    • It is formed in within and in relation to a field

    • Constantly subject to change

    The Current Study

  • Student data from the University of Auckland physics department.

    Bourdieu's Theortical Model (Simplified)

    Comparing tranverse/vertical movements between female and male students

  • Transverse Movements = changing from one field to another.

  • Vertical Movements = moving upwards or downwards in the objectified rankings of the field.

    • Transverse Movements

  • Female students more likely to take life science/chemistry after first year physics

  • Male students more likely to study physics, maths, engineering

  • Original study intention is important. Physics for Life Sciences (Medicine/Bioscience majors) versus Advancing Physics (Physics majors)

    • Vertical Movements

  • Vertical movements upwards require an increase in the prized capital in the field (e.g., science capital in the field of science)

  • Transverse movements can be used as a strategy to protect a vertical position.

    • - When an individual feels that they are slipping in the ranks of the field, they may choose to make a transverse movement to a new field, where their accumulated capital holds more value.

    Physics is often perceived as a domain that is unwelcomming for female students

    Many of the obstacles present in physics, are not as great in the life sciences

  • Negative gender stereotypes (Marchand & Taasoobshirazi, 2013)

  • Chilly climate (Barthelemy, McCormick & Henderson, 2016)

  • Lack of career enhancing resources (Ivie, Tesfaye, Czujko & Chu, 2013)
